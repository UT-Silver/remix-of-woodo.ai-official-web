Three Open Questions
1. Is the capex cycle self-sustaining? ▼

$660–690B capex on a bet AI revenue justifies over 5–10 yrs. Assets depreciate ~20%/yr → $400B annual depreciation by 2030. Need sustained 30%+ cloud AI growth. Bull: demand > supply, record backlogs. Risk: macro slowdown, model plateau, or efficiency breakthrough (DeepSeek). Canary: hyperscaler FCF — if deeply negative across multiple cos simultaneously, market prices in capex cuts.

2. How much share can custom ASICs capture? ▼

ASIC shipments +44.6% vs GPU +16.1% in 2026. NVDA share: 87% → 75%. But TAM expanding $242B → $1.2T (2030) — NVDA absolute rev can grow even as share falls. Key Q: do ASICs stay supplementary (15–25%) or become primary (40%+)? Google TPU → Anthropic deal is strongest evidence for external ASIC traction. Watch: inference cost curves — economics favor cheaper specialized chips.

3. Which layer captures durable value? ▼

Today: NVDA captures most absolute profit. Historically, value migrates upward: hardware → platform → application. Cloud followed this (Dell/HPE compressed → AWS/Azure monopoly → SaaS highest multiples). If AI follows same pattern, long-term winners = application layer (Copilot, AI coding, AI ads) + platform (cloud). NVDA moat (CUDA, full-stack, annual cadence) deeper than any prior HW co — migration timeline may be longer. But gravity → software (marginal cost ≈ 0, high switching costs).
